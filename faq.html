Products & Equipment

We currently offer three products: the Ice Bath NZ (NZ$1,299), our flagship full-body stainless steel ice bath with integrated chilling system; the Chiller (NZ$899), a standalone chilling unit compatible with most tubs; and the Sauna (NZ$449), our insulated entry-level option for those starting their cold journey.

The Arctic Steel Pro and Frost Chiller can achieve temperatures from 2°C to 35°C, with precision held to ±0.5°C. This covers the full therapeutic range used by elite athletes (2–4°C) through to contrast therapy protocols (up to 35°C).

The Portable Pod relies on ice or cold water to achieve temperature — it doesn't have an active chilling system, but with adequate ice can reach 6–8°C in most conditions.

The Arctic Steel Pro holds 450 litres, designed for full-body immersion up to shoulder depth for users up to 200cm tall. The Portable Pod holds 220 litres — suitable for most users up to 185cm with legs drawn in.

No plumbing is required for any of our products. The Arctic Steel Pro is a self-contained system — fill it with a standard hose, connect the chilling unit to a standard 240V power outlet, and you're ready. The built-in filtration, circulation, and drainage systems are all self-contained.

A standard waterproof outdoor power outlet (GPO) near your installation point is all you need. We recommend a weatherproof outlet for outdoor setups.

Yes — all our products are designed for outdoor use and tested for New Zealand weather conditions including UV exposure, rain, and humidity. The 304-grade stainless steel is fully weatherproof. The chilling unit should be positioned under cover or with a weatherproof cover in prolonged heavy rain.

The triple-wall insulation on the Arctic Steel Pro holds temperature for up to 72 hours without active chilling, depending on ambient temperature. In typical NZ conditions (15–20°C ambient), you'll see temperature rise of approximately 1–2°C per day when the chiller is off.

02

Cold Therapy & Science

Research suggests 2–4 minutes at 10–15°C, or 1–2 minutes at 4–8°C, is sufficient to trigger the primary physiological responses. Total weekly cold exposure of 11–15 minutes across 3–5 sessions appears to be the threshold for meaningful adaptation.

Beginners should start with 30–60 seconds at a comfortable temperature (12–15°C) and build gradually. More time isn't always better — quality and consistency of exposure matters more than duration. See our Benefits page for full protocol guidance.

For recovery and inflammation reduction: 10–15°C. For mental resilience and norepinephrine release: 4–10°C. For elite athletic recovery protocols: 2–4°C.

The key is to find a temperature that is challenging but manageable — research by Dr. Andrew Huberman and Dr. Susanna Søberg suggests that "uncomfortably cold but safe" is the sweet spot for hormonal response. We recommend starting at 15°C and reducing by 1–2°C per week as you adapt.

Morning cold immersion (within 2 hours of waking) maximises the cortisol and adrenaline response, which can enhance alertness and mood throughout the day. It also aligns with your body's natural cortisol peak.

If your primary goal is post-training recovery, immerse within 30–60 minutes of a high-intensity training session. Avoid cold immersion immediately after strength training if hypertrophy is your primary goal — it may blunt the anabolic response.

Cold immersion is contraindicated for individuals with certain cardiovascular conditions, Raynaud's syndrome, cold urticaria, or uncontrolled hypertension. Consult your physician before starting if you have any cardiovascular, respiratory, or immune conditions.

General safety guidelines: never immerse alone, avoid alcohol before immersion, enter slowly to allow your body to adjust, and always warm up gradually after (no hot showers immediately — gentle movement first). If you feel chest pain, difficulty breathing, or extreme numbness, exit immediately.

Some research suggests that cold immersion immediately after resistance training may attenuate the acute inflammatory response needed for muscle protein synthesis. If your primary goal is muscle hypertrophy, wait 4–6 hours after strength training before immersing, or use cold therapy on non-training days.

For endurance athletes, recovery-focused athletes, and those prioritising metabolic health or mental resilience, this tradeoff is generally not a concern. The overall benefits of consistent cold exposure far outweigh this modest effect for most people.

Shipping & Delivery

We deliver nationwide across New Zealand, including the South Island and rural areas. For the Portable Pod, we also offer delivery to Australia — contact us for a quote.

Large items (Arctic Steel Pro, Frost Chiller) are delivered by freight carrier with a two-person tail-lift delivery to your kerb. Please ensure clear vehicle access to your property on delivery day.

Most North Island metro orders (Auckland, Wellington, Hamilton) are delivered within 2–4 business days. South Island and rural areas are typically 4–7 business days.

Orders are dispatched within 1–2 business days of payment confirmation. You'll receive tracking details by email once dispatched.

Free shipping is included on all orders to New Zealand metro addresses. Rural delivery surcharges apply to some remote areas — these are calculated at checkout and are typically NZ$40–80 depending on location.

All prices shown on our website are inclusive of GST.

We accept returns within 14 days of delivery for unused, undamaged products in original packaging. Return freight costs are the responsibility of the customer unless the product is faulty.

If your product arrives damaged or faulty, please contact us within 48 hours of delivery with photos and we will arrange a free replacement or full refund. We're subject to New Zealand Consumer Guarantees Act requirements and take our obligations seriously.

Warranty & Support

The Arctic Steel Pro and Frost Chiller come with a 2-year manufacturer's warranty covering all defects in materials and workmanship under normal use conditions. The Portable Pod carries a 1-year warranty.

Warranty covers: stainless steel tub and weld integrity, chilling unit components, insulation performance, drainage system, and all electrical components. It does not cover damage from misuse, accidental damage, or cosmetic wear.

Email us at hello@theicebathnz.co.nz with your order number and a description of the issue, including photos or video if possible. We'll assess your claim within 2 business days and arrange repair, replacement, or refund as appropriate.

We keep replacement parts and units in stock in Auckland for fast turnaround — most warranty replacements are resolved within 5–7 business days.

The built-in filtration and UV sanitation system on the Arctic Steel Pro keeps water clean for 4–6 weeks with regular use. We recommend a full drain and clean every 4–6 weeks using a mild pH-neutral cleaner — avoid bleach or harsh acids on stainless steel.

For daily maintenance: skim debris from the surface, check the filter cartridge monthly, and run the circulation pump for at least 15 minutes after each use to turn over the water volume. Full maintenance guide is included with your product and available on request.

Yes — every order includes a setup call with our team (by phone or video) to walk you through installation, first fill, and first use. Most customers are up and running within a couple of hours of delivery.

For Auckland-based customers, we offer in-home installation assistance for large orders. Contact us when you place your order to arrange this.
